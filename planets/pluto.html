Solar System

Pluto was considered a planet from 1930 to 2006, when it was reclassified as a dwarf planet. Before that it was the ninth planet of our solar system. It is so small that it only takes up half of the size of the United States.

Mythology

Pluto was named after Pluto, the god of the underworld, also known to the greeks as Hades. It was suggested by an 11-year old school girl from England, and was accepted due to how Pluto is a dark and distant planet, much like the underworld, which Pluto is the god of. In mythology, Pluto kidnapped Ceres' daughter and took her as his bride. Ceres' daughter, Prosperina, then became the queen of the underworld and ruled alongside Pluto. The greeks feared Hades because of his connection with death, but Pluto was respected by the romans.
