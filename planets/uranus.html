Uranus

Greek Name Roman Name God Of:
Uranus Caelus Sky

Solar System

Uranus is the seventh planet to the sun. As opposed to the other planets, it appears to spin sideways. It is a very cold planet and considered an ice giant. Uranus is unable to be seen with the naked human eye, so it was the first planet discovered via a telescope.

Mythology

Uranus is the only planet named after a greek god, Uranus, known to the romans as Caelus. This is because the ancient romans did not know Uranus existed because they could not see it in the sky. Originally, Uranus was almost named after King George III, but Johann Bode named it to align with the other mythologically named planets. In mythology, Uranus is the titan of the sky and the son of Gaia.
