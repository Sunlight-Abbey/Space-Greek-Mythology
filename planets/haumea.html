Solar System

Haumea is a dwarf planet outside of the orbit of Neptune. It is the quickest planet to make a rotation, only taking 4 hours. However, it takes about 285 earth years to make a full orbit. It is located in the Kuiper belts, and is the only Kuiper Belt object to have rings.

Mythology

Instead of being named after a greek or roman god, Haumea is named after the goddess of ferility and childbirth in Hawaiian Mythology. Unlike Roman and Greek, which have very similar mythologies, Hawaiian mythology does not have direct equivalents to them. Being the goddess of childbirth and fertility, Haumea could possibly be connected to Demeter/Ceres, the goddess of agriculture and motherhood, or Hera/Juno, the goddess of fertility and marriage. Additionally, she could also be the equivalent of Aphrodite, another goddess of fertility. In Hawaiian mythology, she uses magic to convert herself from an old woman to a young woman in order to reproduce with her own offspring.
