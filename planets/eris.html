Eris

Greek Name Roman Name God Of:
Eris Discordia Chaos

Solar System

Eris is one of the largest dwarf planets, about the same size as Pluto. It was discovered in 2005 and is the reason why Pluto eventually became demoted to a dwarf planet. Eris is a little smaller than Earth's moon.

Mythology

Eris is named after the greek goddess of chaos, Eris. She is most well known for indirectly starting the Trojan War. Eris was not invited to a wedding of Achilles' parents. Angered, she threw a golden apple to a man named Paris and told him to give it to the most beautiful goddess. Hera, Aphrodite, and Athena all fought over the title. Eventually, Paris gave it to Aphrodite, the goddess of beauty, and she rewarded him by kidnapping Helen. This caused the Trojan War between the Greeks and the Trojans. It is fitting that the dwarf planet named after the goddess of chaos is the reason Pluto got demoted.
