Greek Name Roman Name God Of:

Solar System

Jupiter is the biggest planet in our solar system, alongwith the oldest. If Jupiter was hollow, 1,000 Earths could fit inside. Even though it is the biggest and oldest, it only takes about 9.9 hours for it to complete a rotation.

Mythology

In mythology, Jupiter was the king of the gods, also known as Zeus to the greeks. Because it was the biggest planet, the ancient romans named it after their most important god. Jupiter symbolized law and order to the romans, ensuring they lived in a productive society. Jupiter was thought to use his thunderbolts to strike justice. In mythology, Jupiter was the brother of Neptune and Pluto.
