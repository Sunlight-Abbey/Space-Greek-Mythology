Bootes

The Constellation

Bootes is a constellation representing a herdsman or farmer. It is located in the northern sky and is commonly associated with guarding the nearby constellation Ursa Major. Its brightest star is Arcturus, one of the brightest stars visible from Earth.

Mythology

In Greek Mythology, Boötes is believed to represent a herdsman or farmer, sometimes identified as Arcas, the son of Zeus and Callisto. He was known for guiding and watching over the bear, represented by Ursa Major. To honor him, he was placed in the sky as the Boötes constellation.
