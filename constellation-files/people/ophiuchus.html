Ophiuchus

The Constellation

Ophiuchus is a constellation representing a healer, often linked to the figure Asclepius, the god of medicine. It is located near the celestial equator and is sometimes considered a "13th zodiac" constellation, though it is not officially part of the zodiac signs.

Mythology

In Greek Mythology, Ophiuchus represents Asclepius, the god of medicine and healing. He was so skilled that he could even bring the dead back to life. This angered Zeus, who struck him down but later honored him by placing him in the sky holding a serpent, forming the Ophiuchus constellation.
