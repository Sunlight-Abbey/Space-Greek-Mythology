Andromeda

The Constellation

Andromeda is a constellation representing a princess from Greek mythology. It is located in the northern sky and is closely connected to the story of Perseus. Andromeda is best known for containing the Andromeda Galaxy, one of the closest galaxies to Earth.

Mythology

In Greek Mythology, Andromeda was the daughter of King Cepheus and Queen Cassiopeia. After her mother angered the gods by boasting of her beauty, Andromeda was chained to a rock as a sacrifice to the sea monster Cetus. The hero Perseus rescued her before she could be killed. To honor her story, she was placed among the stars as the Andromeda constellation.
