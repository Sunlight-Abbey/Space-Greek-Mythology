Auriga

The Constellation

Auriga is a constellation representing a charioteer. It is located in the northern sky and is often associated with a mythological figure who drove a chariot. Its brightest star is Capella, one of the brightest stars in the night sky.

Mythology

In Greek Mythology, Auriga is often associated with Erichthonius, an early king of Athens who invented the chariot. He was known for his skill and intelligence. After his death, Zeus placed him in the sky as the Auriga constellation, often shown holding a goat.
