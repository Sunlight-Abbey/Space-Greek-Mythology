Cepheus

The Constellation

Cepheus is a constellation representing a king in Greek Mythology. It is located in the northern sky and is part of the story involving his daughter and Andromead. Cepheus is a relatively faint constellation but holds an important place in mythology.

Mythology

In Greek Mythology, Cepheus was the king of Ethiopia and the father of Andromeda. He was part of the myth involving her sacrifice to Cetus. After his death, he was placed among the stars as the Cepheus constellation, alongside his family.
