Aquila

The Constellation

Aquila is a constellation representing an eagle. It is located along the Milky Way and is associated with Zeus, who used the eagle as a emssenger. Its brightest start is Altair, part of the Summer Triangle.

Mythology

In Greek Mythology, Aquila was the eagle of Zeus. It was sent to carry out his orders, including bringing Ganymede, another constellation, to Mount Olympus. In order to honor its service, Zeus placed the eagle in the sky when it died as the Aquila constelltation.
