Aquarius

The Constellation

Aquarius is one of the 12 zodiac signs, and one of the three air signs. Even though it has the root "Aqua" in it, despite popular belief, it is not a water sign. Aquarius' symbol is two waves above each other, known as the water bearer. Its dates are in between January 20th and February 18th. Its ruling planet is Saturn. It's the eleventh zodiac sign. Fun Fact: Michael Jordan is an aquarius.

Mythology

In Greek Mythology, Ganymede was the son of Trojan king, Tros. He was considered a very handsome Trojan Prince. He was taken to Mount Olympus by Zeus, shaped like an eagle, to act as a cup-bearer to the gods. He was put into the constellations when he died alongside his cup. He was placed next to the constellation Aquila, who some consider the eagle that carried him to Mount Olympus.
