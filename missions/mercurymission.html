Mission

Project Mercury was the first in-man space mission. It made six manned flights from 1961 to 1963, starting in 1958. It had 7 people on its crew. The first U.S spaceflight happened in May of 1961, its objective to determine man's capabilities in a space environment and to get a human safely back to Earth.

Mythology

Mercury was the roman god of speed and the messenger of the gods. NASA named their mission after him to signify their goal of rapidly getting man into space, beating the Soviet Union during the Space Race. He was the son of Jupiter and Maia, another spacecraft. He is the roman equivalent of the greek god, Hermes.
