Mission

Hiten launched in January of 1990. Its goal was to test technologies for future missions, its target being Earth's moon. It was Japan's earth orbiting satellite. It passed by the moon 10 times before being purposefully crashed April of 1993.

Mythology

In Japanese Buddhism, hiten refers to celestial beings. It is named after heavenly beings to symbolize a spacecraft reaching towards the heavens and the moon. It came from India and then moved through Central Asia until becoming a major element in Japan.
