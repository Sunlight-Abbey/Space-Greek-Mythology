Mission

Suzaku launched in July of 2005. It ended in September of 2015. It was a space satellite that studied high-energy X-rays, such as supernova explosions and black holes. It is Japan's fifth X-ray astronomy mission. It was developed at the Institute of Space of Japan and collaborated with NASA.

Mythology

Suzaku is a celestial guardian bird. It connects back to the space mission due to its high energy, like what the space satellite studies. In many ways it resembles an asian version of the Phoenix. It represents fire and summer. It guards the Southern sky and embodies virtue.
