Mission

Oracles launched in August of 2016, aiming to study how airborne particles change cloud properties. Its two goals are to determine the impact of aerosol on cloud properties and the radiation balance over the South Atlantic, as well as to acquire a process-level understanding of aerosol-cloud-radiation interactions.

Mythology

In Greek mythology, the oracle was a person who acted as a direct portal for divine communication, typically delivering and acting as a prophet. The most famous Oracle was the Oracle of Delphi. The reason why the Oracle was named after this is because it serves to keep data and answer any question, like the greek oracle did. A popular Oracle was Cassandra, a woman who told true phrophecies but was cursed to never be believed. She was a Trojan princess who predicted the fall of Troy, and warned the Trojans against the Trojan Horse, but was not believed and dismissed as insane.
