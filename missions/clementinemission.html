Clementine Mission

Mission

Clementine was designed to test spacecraft components during extended space exposure, as well to study the moon and asteroids. It succeeded in its moon mission, but the asteroid flyby got cancelled. Clementine provided our first complete look of all the poles of the moon. It launched in January of 1994 and the mission was terminated in August of 1994.

Mythology

Clementia is the roman goddess of mercy. The NASA Clementine was most likely named after it because it contrasted the harsh and cold view of space. Clementia was strongly worshipped by Julius Caesar. Whenever he was benevolent towards his enemies, his policies were known as clementia and were highly celebrated.
