Artemis Mission

Mission

Since 1972, nobody has stepped on the moon. The Artemis program aims to remedy this, aiming to send more people to the moon by 2028. It also plans on establishing a permanent base on it. It's first flight was in 2022, and the first crew flight was April of 2026.

Mythology

The Artemis mission is named after the greek goddess, Artemis. Artemis was a greek goddess associated with the moon, along with hunting and young women. She is the twin sister of Apollo, the god of sunlight and the namesake of the mission that first brought people to the moon in 1969-1972. In mythology, Artemis was associated with Selene, the greek goddess personification of the moon, like how Apollo gets associated with Helios.
