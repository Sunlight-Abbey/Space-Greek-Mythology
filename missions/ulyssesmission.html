Mission

Ulysses launched in October of 1990, surveying the space environment above and below the sun poles. It made three orbits of the sun during 18+ years of service. It showed that there is a weakening of the solar wind over time, the solar magnetic field at the poles is weaker, and the sun's magnetic field reverses every 11 years.

Mythology

In Roman Mythology, Ulysses' voyage home took 20 years due to all the problems he faced. The spacecraft was named after him due to the extensive time it would take. Ulysses was the king of Ithaca, and the roman equivalent of Odysseus. In Greek mythology, Odysseus faced the cyclops and poseidon. Then, he got trapped on Calypso's island for 7 years. This delayed his journey home by a significant amount.
