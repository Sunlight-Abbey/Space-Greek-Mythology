Mission Name

Mission

Helios's mission was to investigate the sun and the solar environment. The first one launched in December 1974, while the second launched in January 1976. The spacecraft was put into heliocentric orbit and flew 27 million miles, the record holder at the time for the closest flight to the sun. It provided important info on solar plasma, cosmic rays, solar wind, and cosmic dust.

Mythology

Helios was the greek god and personification of the sun. The reason why the mission is named after him is self-explanatory, because it's about the sun. Helios was a titan, however during the titan and olympus war he sided with the olympians. Due to his siding with them, Zeus allowed him to keep his job as the sun god. He is well known for driving his sun chariot, pulled by four horses.
