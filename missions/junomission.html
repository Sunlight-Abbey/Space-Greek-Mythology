Mission

Juno launched in August of 2011 in order to explore the jovian system. It arrived at Jupiter in 2016, probing beneath the dense clouds encircling the planet. Its goal is to find out about the origin and evolution of Jupiter. it has provided breathtaking images and breakthrough discovers from Jupiter and its moons, sharing these pictures with the world and fueling citizen science.

Mythology

In Roman mythology, Juno is the wife of Jupiter. This is the reason it was named after her, due to its probing of Jupiter and its dense clouds. Juno was the queen of the gods and the goddess of marriage, womnen, and childbirth. Her sacred animal is the peacock. In a lot of myths, Juno is portrayed as a jealous and bitter wife. This could be connected back to the spacecraft, as it checking on Jupiter could relate to Juno checking on Jupiter to make sure he is not having an affair.
