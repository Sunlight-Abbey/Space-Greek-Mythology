Calipso Mission

Mission

The CALIPSO (Cloud-Aerosol Lidar and Infrared Pathfinder Satellite Observations) was created to study the impact of clouds and aerosols on Earth's atmosphere and climate. It measured over 10 billion light measurements to measure the altitude and properties of clouds.

Mythology

In Greek Mythology, Calypso is an ocean nymph who lives on the island of Ogygia. Ogygia is an island hidden far away from humanity, kept away from human contact. Like the hidden island, the CALIPSO works on finding hidden particles in the Earth's atmopshere. Fun Fact: Calypso is considered a descendant of Atlas, which is also the name of another NASA mission. Calypso is most famously known for her role in the Odyssey, keeping Odysseus hidden away on her island for 7 years.
