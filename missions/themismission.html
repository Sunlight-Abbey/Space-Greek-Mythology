Mission

Themis launched February of 2007 in order to study what initiates auroras near Earth's poles. It studies how mass and energy move through the near-Earth space environment. After it completed its prime mission in 2010, it was sent into orbit around the moon to begin measurements of conditions in the distant tail of Earth's magnetic environment.

Mythology

Themis was named after the greek goddess of justice, wisdom, and counsel. It was designed to judge or determine the physical processes behind magnetic substorms, relating back to the goddess' sense of council. Themis was one of the twelve titan children of Gaia, another spacecraft, and the second wife of Zeus. She was also associated with oracles, another spacecraft. She was the first recorded appearance of justice as a divine personage.
