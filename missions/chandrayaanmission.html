Chandrayaan Mission

Mission

The Chandrayaan Program was created by the Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO) and is intended to explore the moon. While Chandrayaan-3 was successful, Chandrayaan-2 crashed on the surface. This made India the first country to land a spacecraft in the Lunar South Pole Region.

Mythology

Chandrayaan means Moon Vehicle, stemming from the word Chandra. Chandra is a hindu deity of the moon, shown as a youthful god riding a chariot pulled by ten white horses. He is believed to nurture plants and bestow mental peace. His connection with the moon in India is the reason the Chandrayaan was named after him and his vehicle. Chandra is one of the nine celestial bodies that govern planetary influence.
