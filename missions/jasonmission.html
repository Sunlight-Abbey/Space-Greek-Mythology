Mission

Jason as launched in December of 2001 in order to monitor sea surface levels and wave heights. It uses two satellites to measure Earth's gravity field. Its objectives are to provide a 5-year view of global ocean surface topography, increase understanding of ocean circulation, and improve climate forecasting.

Mythology

Jason was named after the greek hero, Jason, who led the argonauts on a quest to find the Golden Fleece. This name symbolizes the satellites' mission to explore and map the world's ocean. The Jason satellites navigates the seas from space to find precise measurements of sea surfaec height. He was the son of a king and married to a sorceress, the granddaughter of Helios, another spacecraft.
