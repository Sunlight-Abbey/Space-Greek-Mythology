Mission

TOPEX/Poseidon launched in August of 1992 in order to obtain global measurements of sea-surface heights. It allowed scientists to forecast the 1997-1998 El Nino, a natural climate pattern where the ocean surface temperatures warm and disrupts global weather. By its end in 2006, it had monitored large-scale ocean features, effects on global climate change, and mapped changes in heat stored.

Mythology

In Greek mythology, Poseidon was the god of the sea and storms. The reason they named it after him is due to their measurements of sea-surface heights. Poseidon ruled the seas while his brothers, Zeus and Hades, ruled the skies and underworld respectively. Poseidon had the most children in all of greek mythology, ranging from nymphs to humans to cyclops. The most famous cyclops, Polyphemus, is a son of Poseidon. Poseidon is well known in the Odyssey for extending Odysseus' trip home, making it last 10 years.
